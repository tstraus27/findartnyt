Kitten pointer look mechanics

Neutral/front is the peaceful idle. Rows 9-10 are pointer engagement: Kitten turns toward the cursor and becomes naughty-aggressive, using the hiss/bite expression from the reference photo.

The natural motion is face-first tracking: green eyes and pupils lead, the nose and black-lipped underbite follow, ears pin or flare toward the target, and one paw lifts as if ready to swat. The compact body and feet stay stable so the sprite does not spin or slide. Do not rotate the whole cat as an object.

Cardinal pose families:

- 000 up: eyes and nose aim upward, ears perk/pin high, black lips part and the lower canine/underbite remain visible.
- 090 screen-right: eyes, nose, muzzle, and hiss-bite mouth pull toward screen-right; screen-right cheek/ear lead.
- 180 down: eyes and nose aim downward, head tucks into a suspicious bite-ready crouch, teeth still visible.
- 270 screen-left: eyes, nose, muzzle, and hiss-bite mouth pull toward screen-left; screen-left cheek/ear lead.

Intermediates should smoothly interpolate through these bitey target-tracking poses. Preserve grey/silver tabby coloring, pale cream muzzle and chest, green eyes, orange nose, black lips, huge underbite, upward lower canine, tiny lower teeth, thick dark outline, chunky pixel-sprite readability, and cute-but-aggressive hover personality.
